## 1. Modernization — SDLC is Over. ADLC is in the Room.

Traditional SDLC assumes humans write every line. **ADLC** assumes agents draft, test, and refactor — and humans review, decide, and own.

**Skills that actually matter in ADLC:**

- **Spec-driven development (SDD)** — the spec is the source of truth, code is generated artifact
- **Prompt & context engineering** — treating prompts as versioned, tested assets
- **Agent orchestration** — knowing when to use one agent vs. a team
- **Evaluation-first thinking** — you can't ship what you can't measure
- **Legacy fluency** — reading COBOL, .NET, and old React well enough to guide the agent

We embed with your teams and upskill them while we modernize the stack.

---

## 2. QE Automation — Testing at the Speed of AI

Your delivery is faster. Your QE has to be faster too. We build AI-native quality engineering pipelines that stop being the bottleneck.

- **Test data generation** — synthetic, compliant, production-shaped datasets on demand
- **Test scenario mining** — agents surface edge cases from logs, specs, and user behavior
- **Script generation** — executable tests written from requirements, not after
- **Chatbot & conversational AI testing** — semantic, multi-turn, adversarial coverage

Outcome: higher coverage, lower maintenance, and QE that ships with the release — not after it.

---

## 3. Agent Validation — Trust Your Agents in Production

Most teams ship agents and then discover the next-step decisions don't match what the business actually wanted. We fix that.

- **Agent observability** — trace every decision, every tool call, every handoff
- **Next-step evaluation** — did the agent pick the right action, not just a plausible one?
- **Regression & drift detection** — catch behavior changes before customers do
- **ADK framework setup** — stand up **Google ADK** as your agent platform

We'll set you up on the agent toolchain that fits your stack — **Claude Code, Cursor, GitHub Copilot,** or **Google Antigravity** — and make sure the agents you build are actually ready for production.
