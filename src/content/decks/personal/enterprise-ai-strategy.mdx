# Enterprise AI Strategy 2026

## Building Intelligent Systems That Scale

*A practical framework for technology leaders*

---

## The AI Maturity Gap

Most enterprises are stuck between **experimentation** and **production**.

- 87% of ML models never reach production
- Average time from prototype to deployment: 9 months
- Data teams spend 80% of time on prep, not innovation

**The gap isn't talent — it's architecture.**

---

## The Four Pillars

### 1. Data Foundations
Clean, governed, accessible data platforms

### 2. ML Infrastructure
Reproducible training, versioned models, automated pipelines

### 3. Deployment & Monitoring
Real-time serving, drift detection, A/B testing

### 4. Organization & Culture
Cross-functional teams, AI literacy, ethical frameworks

---

## Pillar 1: Data Foundations

- **Lakehouse architecture** — best of data lakes and warehouses
- **Data contracts** — schema enforcement between producers and consumers
- **Quality gates** — automated data validation at ingestion
- **Catalog & lineage** — know what data exists and where it flows

> Without solid data foundations, AI is built on sand.

---

## Pillar 2: ML Infrastructure

- Feature stores for consistent feature computation
- Experiment tracking (MLflow, W&B)
- Model registry with approval workflows
- GPU orchestration for cost-efficient training

**Key metric:** Time from experiment to production model

---

## Pillar 3: Deploy & Monitor

- Containerized model serving (KServe, Triton)
- Shadow deployments and canary releases
- Real-time drift detection and alerting
- Automated retraining triggers

**Observability is not optional — it's foundational.**

---

## Pillar 4: Organization

- Embed ML engineers in product teams
- Executive AI literacy programs
- Responsible AI review boards
- Clear metrics: business impact, not just model accuracy

---

## 90-Day Quickstart

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |

| Assess | 1-3 | Audit data assets, identify 3 high-value use cases |
