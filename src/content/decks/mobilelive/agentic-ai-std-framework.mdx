## What is Agentic AI?

AI systems that **act autonomously** — not just respond to prompts, but perceive their environment, reason about goals, and take action.

- Goes beyond chatbots and copilots
- Agents plan multi-step workflows independently
- They use tools, call APIs, and make decisions
- Human oversight at critical checkpoints, not every step

> Agentic AI is the shift from AI as a tool to AI as a collaborator.

---

## The STD Framework

A structured approach to designing enterprise-grade AI agents:

### Sense
Perceive and ingest data from the environment — APIs, documents, events, user inputs

### Think
Reason, plan, and decide — leveraging LLMs, knowledge graphs, and business rules

### Do
Execute actions — API calls, database writes, notifications, workflow triggers

---

## Sense: Perception Layer

Agents must understand their operating context:

- **API monitoring** — real-time ingestion of system events and webhooks
- **Document understanding** — extract structured data from PDFs, emails, tickets
- **User intent parsing** — natural language instructions mapped to agent capabilities
- **Environment awareness** — system state, thresholds, SLA timers

The richer the perception, the smarter the agent.

---

## Think: Reasoning Engine

Where intelligence lives:

- **LLM-powered planning** — break complex goals into executable steps
- **Tool selection** — choose the right API, database, or service for each step
- **Guardrails** — policy enforcement, budget limits, compliance checks
- **Memory** — short-term (conversation context) and long-term (learned patterns)
- **Reflection** — agents evaluate their own outputs before acting

---

## Do: Execution Layer

Turning decisions into outcomes:

- **Tool use** — REST APIs, database queries, file operations
- **Multi-step orchestration** — chained actions with error handling and retries
- **Human-in-the-loop** — escalation when confidence is low or stakes are high
- **Audit trail** — every action logged for compliance and debugging
